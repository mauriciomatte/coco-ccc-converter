# K7 Tab — Cassette Tape

The **K7** tab reads, tunes, edits, decodes and converts CoCo/Dragon **cassette tapes**. It is where you
**recover** programs from old tapes, **preserve** a tape as a clean file, **convert** loader games, and
**test** everything in an embedded mini-emulator. By the end of this guide you will know which button to use
in each situation and what each one delivers.

---

## 1. What you can open
- **WAV** — the digitized tape audio (what you capture from a recorder).
- **CAS / C10** — the digital image (the tape bytes) used by emulators.
- **VOC** — old audio (Creative Voice File).

Use **Open** (in the toolbar) or **drag** the file onto the waveform area. While dragging, a **dashed
overlay** appears: "Drop the tape (.wav/.cas/.voc/.c10) to open". When you open a `.cas/.c10`, the app
already turns on **"Fast read"** and zooms into a small window so you can see the FSK square waves.

While the file is decoded, a **loading bar** with a percentage appears over the waveform ("Loading tape…
42%"). If something goes wrong (unsupported format, invalid WAV/VOC, CAS→WAV failed), the **error message**
shows in yellow in the center of the waveform area (when no tape is loaded) or in the status bar. Accepted
formats: **.WAV** (PCM 8/16-bit), **.CAS**, **.C10** and **.VOC** (Creative Voice File).

---

## 2. The waveform

The waveform is drawn with a **time ruler**, a yellow **playhead** and zoom. In the top-right corner are:
a **zoom badge** ("2.0×", the current level), the **drawing style** button and the **zoom in/out** buttons.
The style button cycles 4 modes:
- **line** — oscilloscope; the only one that shows the **real shape** (the FSK square wave). Use it zoomed in.
- **bars** (default) — min→max stroke per column; **peak** (filled envelope); **RMS** (energy) — good for
  viewing the whole tape.

**Zoom:** mouse wheel (centered on the cursor), zoom in/out buttons (the "zoom out" disables when everything
is already visible), and an orange **scrollbar** that appears **only when zoomed** (drag to scroll back and
forth). **Selection:** drag over the waveform to mark a span (highlighted in orange) — that selection feeds
all editing. A **single click** positions the playhead (seek); the cursor turns into a "text" cursor when
a tape is loaded.

### Checkered progress bar (status + seek)
Below the waveform is a **checkered bar** (72 cells, GW-tab style), flanked by the **current time** (left)
and the **total length** (right). It shows how much of the tape has played and is **clickable**: click
anywhere to **seek** the playhead there.

---

## 3. The player and the animated cassette

Below the waveform is an **animated datacorder**: the reels spin during playback (and during recording).
The controls, left to right:
- **Rewind** — back to the start (and keeps playing if it was playing).
- **Play / Pause** — toggles; the icon changes. While mirroring to the mini-XRoar, Play may enter a
  **waiting** state ("Waiting for XRoar" — up to ~8 s) while the mini types `CLOAD`; click again to
  **cancel** the wait.
- **● REC** (red) — records from line-in/mic (section 10).
- **■ Stop** — stops and rewinds to start; during recording, it **ends the recording**.
- **Fast-forward** — jumps straight to the **end** of the tape.
- **Eject** (up arrow) — clears the loaded waveform (**asks for confirmation**, section 10).

Next to them is a time **counter** (shows the current time; turns **green** while playing, **red** while
recording, and shows "XRoar…" while waiting for the mini-emulator) and the **Speed** selector:
- **Epoch** — the CoCo's real speed **measured** from the file itself (the selector shows the factor in the
  label, e.g. "Epoch (1.03×)"). The recommended one, already selected.
- **Normal** (1×), **-2× / -4×** (slows down, helps inspection), **×2 / ×4** (speeds up).

While recording, a **● REC** indicator with a **VU** meter (input level) also appears on the right — green,
turning red when the signal saturates (near clip).

It is all monitoring — it **does not change the data**.

---

## 4. Decode — see the tape's programs

On open, the app **demodulates the FSK** automatically and shows it in the **"Program"** panel (right
column). By default the panel is **collapsed**, showing only the **file** (name, truncated) and the
**extension**, plus an orange **"more info / less"** button that **expands/collapses** the rest. When
collapsed, the button still shows a green **✓** or yellow **✗** indicating whether it synced.

Expanded, the panel lists: **file (system)** (size in bytes of the loaded file), **rate** (kHz), **format**
(bits · mono/channels), **length**, and the decoded program fields — **name, start (load), end, exec, type**
(BASIC/Machine/Data) and **program (CoCo)** (size + the **RAM class** that would fit it: 4K/8K/16K/32K/64K).
Below comes the status: **decoding…**, **✓ sync · N files** (green) or **✗ no sync** (yellow). If the tape
holds several programs, a **list** appears below — click each item to extract that one.

> **Incomplete decode?** If there is far more audio than recovered data, a yellow warning appears
> ("incomplete decode: adjust the sound or use → Full tape"): the tape probably has a **screen + loader**
> (section 7) or is degraded (section 5).

---

## 5. Sound adjustments ≠ Recover — the biggest confusion

In the **"Sound adjustments"** panel (it shows the waveform **Rate (kHz)** at the top, read-only):
- **Threshold (µs)** — the point separating bit 0 from bit 1 (default 600, range 500–720). It has a
  **slider** plus **− / +** buttons (0.5 µs step). **Min amplitude** — ignores weak noise (0 to 0.30,
  slider + **− / +** of 0.01). **Default** (↺) — resets everything to safe (Threshold 600, Amplitude 0,
  WAV 22 kHz). → Use for **MANUAL fine tuning** when the tape almost syncs.
- **Recover** (✨) — turns on the **AUTOMATIC, adaptive** decode: it calibrates the threshold **per segment**
  (Otsu method + sweep) and ignores the manual settings (it shows a **✓** and an orange highlight when on).
  → Use on **degraded tapes** (40+ years, slow, stretched) that give "NO-SYNC".

To the right of the buttons a **counter** "N bits · N B" shows how many bits/bytes the demodulation
extracted, and below it a **dynamic hint** changes depending on whether Recover is on.

**Rule of thumb:** first enable **Recover**; if still incomplete, tweak **Threshold/Amplitude**; if it gets
worse, click **Default** and start over. The app re-decodes on every change (with a small debounce delay).

### 5.1 Advanced recovery (heavily degraded tapes)

Below the sound settings there is the **"Advanced recovery"** panel (only when a tape is open):
- **Effects** (applied to the WAVE — **PLAY** and the **mini-XRoar** now play the result, and the wave shows
  the effect; **Undo** reverts): **DC** (removes offset/drift), **Band** (band-limit 600 Hz–3.5 kHz, kills
  rumble and hiss), **AGC** (lifts weak/dropout regions), **Treble** (compensates a misaligned head/azimuth).
  Apply one at a time, test on PLAY/mini-XRoar and, if it gets worse, click **Undo**.
- **Channel** (stereo captures only): applies channel 0 or 1 to the wave (re-read from the original) — one
  side may be better than the other.
- **Diagnose** — shows the **period histogram** (two peaks ≈417/833 µs; the valley is the threshold, marked
  red) and the per-segment **block map** (green = OK, red = bad) → you see **where** the tape fails.
  **Re-decode selection** redoes only the region you marked on the waveform.
- **Merge captures…** (tape RAID) — record/open **several captures of the SAME tape** and the app combines the
  **best blocks** of each (a block that fails in one passes in another). Includes the currently open tape.

> **Undo/Redo cover EVERYTHING:** waveform edits (cut/paste/normalize/pause), the **effects** above, and also
> the **adjustment** changes (Threshold/Amplitude/Recover). So you can experiment freely and step back.

**Multi-pass (REC):** record the same physical tape several times (REC button), then use **Merge captures** to
combine the best blocks of all passes — the most effective method for physical damage. **Diagnose** shows each
pass's quality before you trust it.

## 6. The live panels: BASIC · HEX · mini-XRoar

Below the player are three panels (adjustable widths):
- **BASIC (detokenized · 32 col)** — shows the BASIC program already **detokenized**, wrapped to 32 columns
  (the CoCo's screen width). During **Play**, the text is revealed "typewriter" style, synced to the
  playhead — you watch the program "load" like back in the day.
- **Hex (program)** — a hex+ASCII dump of the full **raw stream**, also revealed byte by byte as the tape
  plays (header → screen/loader → turbo game).
- **mini-XRoar** — an embedded **muted** mini-emulator. It is **collapsible**: a narrow **side strip** with
  a button (a "screen share" icon) always sits on the right to **open/close** the mini-XRoar (and turn it
  on/off). Open, when you **Play** it resets and runs on a small screen alongside, mirroring the load, with
  the command **matching the program type**: BASIC → **`CLOAD:RUN`**, machine language → **`CLOADM:EXEC`**
  (detected from the tape's namefile). When the mini-XRoar is on, the **"Fast read"** toggle appears **in the toolbar**: ON
  loads the `.cas` quickly (digital files, no animation); OFF plays the WAV in real time (with the real
  gaps, syncing the audio to the typed `CLOAD`, ideal for loader tapes). Opening a `.cas/.c10` turns this
  toggle **on automatically**.

> **Panel widths:** between the panels are **draggable dividers** (splitters). Drag to redistribute space
> among BASIC, HEX and mini-XRoar — the widths are **remembered** across sessions. During Play, the HEX and
> BASIC panels **auto-scroll** to follow the end of the read.

## 7. Tape types — and what to do with each
- **STANDARD tape** (BASIC or ML saved normally): decodes fully → use the export/send buttons.
- **Tape with a SPLASH SCREEN + LOADER** (softhouse games, e.g. SoftKristian): the standard decoder reads
  only the namefile + the loader; the game comes after in a **turbo/custom stream** it doesn't decode (hence
  "incomplete decode"). For these, tweaking the sound won't help — use:
  - **"→ No loader"** (build an autostart BIN/DSK, keeping the screen), or
  - **"→ Full tape"** + run in **XRoar** (loads EVERYTHING like the original tape).

---

## 8. The conversion/export buttons — what each delivers

**Two places** trigger exports: the **icons in the toolbar** (top) and the **"Export"** panel (right
column). At the top of the panel is the **"Tape name (8 chars)"** field: it is the name written into the
tape *namefile* — what the CoCo shows on `LIST` and what appears when you reopen it. It is pre-filled with
the name read from the tape but is **editable** (max 8 chars, A-Z/0-9) and is applied when saving **"→ CAS"**,
**"→ WAV"** and when **Extract**ing in `.cas` format. The "Export" panel also has a **WAV rate (kHz)** selector with 4 options (8 / 11 / 22 / 44 kHz —
"lower = smaller file", safe floor for the FSK ≈ 8 kHz) and a **real-time size preview** (→ CAS clean,
→ WAV clean, → Full tape, and the **original** size) that recomputes when you tweak the sound or the kHz,
so you can compare before saving. A note reminds: *the rate only affects .WAV; the .CAS depends only on
the program.*

| Button | What it does | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **From disk** | **disk → tape**: takes the file **selected** in a disk pane (A/B), packs it into a `.CAS` and opens it here as a tape (playable/editable) — then you can export `.CAS`/`.WAV` | put a program that lives on a disk onto tape |
| **FIXCAS** | opens a `.CAS` from the PC and **validates/repairs** it: recomputes checksums, rebuilds leader/sync and ensures EOF, saving a clean `.CAS` | fix a corrupted `.CAS` (won't run / reads half-way) without touching the loaded waveform |
| **→ CAS** | **Normalize**: writes a CLEAN, tiny `.CAS` with just the decoded data | keep a reliable, small copy of a standard tape |
| **→ WAV** | **Normalize**: writes a CLEAN `.WAV` (≈11 kHz), much smaller, reliable on hardware | play on a real recorder/hardware |
| **→ Full tape** | writes the `.WAV` of the **entire ORIGINAL audio** — header, **screen**, loader and turbo game (22 kHz floor) | tapes with a splash screen that must run fully in XRoar |
| **→ No loader** | opens the **autostart** dialog (LOADM, no `:EXEC`) — removes the tape loader and keeps the screen | turn a loader-tape game into a disk file that runs by itself |
| **Revert** | opens one of OUR `.BIN` files (**CoCo DCU** signature) and recovers the **PURE PROGRAM** as `.CAS`/`.WAV` | undo a "→ No loader" and get just the program |
| **(→ DSK)** | writes the read file (BASIC/ML) into a **DSK pane** | put the tape's content on a disk |
| **(→ XRoar)** | loads the current tape into the **emulator** (XRoar tab) | test the tape right away |
| **(Open in BASIC)** | sends the tape's **BASIC** to the **BASIC editor** (detokenizes) | edit the BASIC program |
| **Extract** (Program panel) | saves the selected program in the format chosen by the adjacent **BIN/BAS/CAS** selector (BAS is **disabled** for non-BASIC files) | pull one specific program off the tape |

> **About the Extract formats:** **.bin** = ML wrapped in a DECB LOADM (runs in XRoar and is LOADM-able;
> BASIC/data come out raw); **.bas** = **detokenized** BASIC text (BASIC files only); **.cas** = the same
> canonical tape as "→ CAS" (playable in XRoar/MAME). On a multi-segment loader tape, the app suggests
> using **→ CAS** if the `.bin` won't run.

> In short: **→ CAS/→ WAV = clean/normalized** (standard part only). **→ Full tape = everything, raw** (for
> screens/loaders). **→ No loader = convert to an autostart disk**. **Revert = the way back**.

> **Safety shortcuts in the toolbar buttons:** the **↔ DSK** first detects whether the tape has a
> screen+loader — if so it does **not** write the raw file (which would land at $009F = garbage), warns you
> and **opens** the "→ No loader" dialog for you. The **→ XRoar** also warns (without blocking) that on a
> loader tape `CLOADM` reads only the stub, and suggests "→ No loader" or the real-time mini-XRoar.

> **From disk (disk → tape):** select a file in a disk pane (A or B) on the main tab and click **From disk**
> here in K7. The app packs the file into a `.CAS` tape (ML becomes the memory image with the correct
> load/exec addresses; BASIC/data go raw) and loads it into the deck — then you can play, edit the waveform
> and export `.CAS`/`.WAV` for a real recorder, FujiNet or XRoar. It's the inverse of **↔ DSK**.

> **FIXCAS (repair a .CAS tape):** opens a `.CAS`/`.C10` from the PC, scans it tolerantly, recovers the
> blocks (even with bad checksums) and **rewrites a canonical tape** — correct leader/sync, **recomputed
> checksums** and a guaranteed **EOF** per file. It then shows a report (files, blocks, checksums fixed,
> false syncs skipped, EOFs added) and saves a clean `.CAS`. It does not change the tape loaded in the
> waveform — it works on the file you pick in the dialog.

### The "→ No loader (autostart)" dialog
It shows the detected loader (name **SoftKristian**, **confidence** in %, whether it has a **512B screen →
$0400**) and the program line (**size** and the **$load–$end** range). You can edit the program's **Load**
and **Exec** in hex, choose the **Start** trigger (**key** or **~10s delay**) and tick **autostart $0176**.
**Yellow warnings (⚠)** flag if the program ends near the **DOS stack** (if the .DSK hangs, use the .BIN) or
overlaps the **DOS-reserved RAM ($0600–$25FF)**. Buttons: **Write to DSK** (inject into a pane; only shown
if a DSK pane is available) or **Save .BIN** (runs with LOADM, no `:EXEC`). Clicking outside or **Cancel**
closes the dialog.

## 9. Editing the waveform

With a span **selected**: **cut / copy / paste / delete / crop-to-selection / normalize amplitude / insert
a pause (1.0 s of silence)**. Useful to trim dead leader/tail, split segments or create gaps between
programs. (Normalizing the amplitude often improves decoding.)

**Undo / Redo (Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y)** work on a **SINGLE history** that records, in order, **everything** that
changes the tape state:
- **waveform edits** (cut, paste, delete, crop, normalize, insert pause);
- the **effects** from the "Advanced recovery" panel (DC, Band, AGC, Treble, Channel) — applied to the wave
  itself, so PLAY and the mini-XRoar then play the result;
- the **adjustment** changes (Threshold, Amplitude, Recover and the **Default** button) — each slider drag
  counts as **one** step.

So you can experiment freely (apply an effect, tweak the threshold, cut a span) and **step back** if something
gets worse — then **step forward** again with Redo. The ↶/↷ buttons are **dimmed** when there is nothing to
undo/redo. (Opening another tape or **ejecting** clears the history.)

---

## 10. Recording from a real recorder (REC)

The **● REC** button **captures** the datacorder's audio via the PC's audio input (line-in): a **● REC**
indicator with a **VU** meter (level, green→red on saturation) appears and the reels spin. Click **■ Stop**
to finish — the audio becomes a waveform (`gravacao.wav`) you decode/edit normally. If a tape is already
open, the app opens a **confirmation dialog** ("Record over?") before recording over it — and warns if there
are **unexported edits**.

> **Tip:** the app disables echo cancellation, noise suppression and auto gain on capture, and **mutes the
> monitor** (no feedback), to preserve the FSK as faithfully as possible.

### Eject (clear the tape)
The **Eject** button (up arrow, on the datacorder) **clears** the current waveform. If a tape is loaded, it
opens the "Eject the tape?" dialog — confirming zeroes everything (waveform, decode, selection, edit
history, clipboard). It warns first if there are unexported edits.

---

## 11. Remembered settings and the "?" button

The tab **persists across sessions** (in the browser): the sound settings (**Threshold**, **Amplitude**),
the chosen **speed**, the **extract format** (BIN/BAS/CAS), the **WAV rate**, the **mini-XRoar mirroring**
state, the **waveform style**, whether the **Program** panel is expanded, and the **widths** of the three
bottom panels. The **?** button (right end of the toolbar) reopens this help at any time.

---

## 12. Typical flows (quick guide)
- **Standard tape, want to preserve it:** open → (Recover if needed) → **→ CAS** (or **→ WAV**).
- **Tape won't sync:** enable **Recover** → adjust **Threshold/Amplitude** → if worse, **Default**.
- **Game with screen + loader (SoftKristian):** open → **→ No loader** (builds autostart BIN/DSK) → test.
- **Screen game that won't convert:** **→ Full tape** → **→ XRoar**.
- **Want the pure program back:** open our `.BIN` with **Revert**.
- **Capture a physical tape:** connect the recorder to line-in → **● REC** → **Stop** → decode → export.
